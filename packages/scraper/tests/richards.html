Violini virtuosa Vicki Richards debuted with the Indian-inspired Parting the Waters (Third Stream, 1989 - Coconut Grove, 1998). Her violin duets with guitar, tablas and other percussion instruments concocting a spiritual fusion of styles, like a jazz version of the Third Ear Band and Popol Vuh, via both evocative watercolors (Endless Radiance, Dance for Jaco, Monsoon) world-music vignettes (Parting the Waters) and avantgarde solos (Skater's Dream, Logarhythm).

It was followed by Temple Dwellers (Temple Street Music, 1999), another meeting of east and west, jazz, raga, new-age and classical, and Time In Between (2000), with Amit Chatterjee.
